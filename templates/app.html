Ocean

The ocean plays a crucial role in regulating Earth’s climate, absorbing over 90% of the excess heat caused by greenhouse gas emissions and about 25% of the carbon dioxide we release. Healthy oceans act as a massive carbon sink, buffering the effects of climate change. However, rising temperatures are causing ocean acidification, coral bleaching, and disrupted marine ecosystems, threatening biodiversity and the livelihoods of millions who depend on fishing and coastal resources. Protecting marine life, reducing plastic pollution, and preserving coastal habitats like mangroves and seagrasses are vital steps toward ocean sustainability and climate resilience.
